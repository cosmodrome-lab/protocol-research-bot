# Protocol Research Doctrine v2.1

## Core Mission
Detect intent, coordination, manipulation and misappropriation.
Not technical failures — forensic evidence of deliberate action.


## Universal Research Framework

Every protocol must first be classified before analysis.

Protocol Groups:
- Layer 1
- Layer 2
- DEX
- Lending
- Oracle
- Bridge
- Staking
- Specialized Infrastructure

Each group may use additional checklists,
but all investigations follow the same doctrine.


## Three Depth Levels

### Level 1 — Snapshot (30 min)
Quick forensic scan. Public signals only.
Output: red flags, key actors, timeline.

### Level 2 — Forensic Review (2-4 hours)
Deep governance and onchain analysis.
Output: evidence chain, actor mapping, risk score.

### Level 3 — Deep Forensic (1-3 days)
Full investigation with cross-protocol correlation.
Output: forensic report, judicial-grade evidence.

## What We Look For
- Insider positioning before governance events
- Coordinated voting without public disclosure
- Treasury drain through legitimate mechanisms
- Narrative manipulation before liquidity events
- Proxy governance capture
- Connected wallets hidden behind DAO structure

## Fact Separation Rules
- On-chain facts: verified, timestamped, immutable
- Off-chain claims: sourced, dated, attributed
- Hypothesis: clearly marked, not presented as fact
- Unknown: explicitly acknowledged

## Confidence Scale
A — On-chain verified
B — Multiple independent sources
C — Single credible source
D — Unverified, plausible
E — Speculation, low confidence

## Power Evolution Principle

Every investigation must answer:

- Who controlled the protocol at genesis?
- Who controls the protocol today?
- Who is expected to control it in the future?

Control shifts are often more important than current ownership.

## Threat Model

Every review must identify:

- Governance threats
- Treasury threats
- Centralization threats
- Regulatory threats
- Operational threats

## Moat Analysis

Every protocol must be evaluated for:

- Technical moat
- Network moat
- Liquidity moat
- Regulatory moat
- Data moat

## Narrative vs Reality

Narrative must be separated from measurable reality.

Required questions:

- What is the protocol narrative?
- What supports it?
- What contradicts it?
- What remains unproven?

## Power Evolution Principle
Control shifts over time. Track who gained and who lost power.
- Initial distribution vs current state
- Key transfer events (governance attacks, token accumulation)
- Velocity of centralization

## Threat Model
- External: oracle manipulation, flash loan attacks
- Internal: team rug, governance capture, treasury drain
- Hybrid: coordinated insider + external exploit

## Moat Analysis
- What protects this protocol from attack
- Where moat is weakest
- Historical moat erosion events
